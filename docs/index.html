Econometric Oracle

Nobel Prize Winning Supply Governance

If the economy grows 3%, there are 3% more coins available for mining; that's monetarism at work. It's a clear and simple rule that will not work for the entire money supply - but has practical application for a percentage of it. What percentage? We estimate 1% of total money in the economy (i.e. M2) my be responsibly supplemented.

Milton Friedman won the Nobel Prize in 1976 for his work Economic Sciences; his theory is refereed to as "monetarism." It's not a complete theory, but all central bankers learn and apply parts of it.

Initial cap: 1% of money supply ("M2") in each conntry

Two year mining period until the cap is reached

New Coins for Mining = % GDP Growth x Coins Outstanding

Distribution of MonetaryCoin Tokens

All MonetaryCoin launches will follow the same pattern. 89% of the MonetaryCoin tokens have been set aside for forging in the 24 months following initial distribution. 10% of coins will be distributed over 180 days, and 10% will be allocated evenly between and among the foundation and creators.

The MonetaryCoin distribition event for the first two national coins will take place between May 30th, 2018 and November 26th, 2018 in 180 separate 24 hour blocks. Participants may send ETH to the smart contract for a 23 hour period and then coins will be ratably distributed in the 24th hour.

There is niether a hard cap nor a soft cap; the project will go ahead regardless of the amount raised. Any unissued coins from a given 23 hour period will be immediately returned to the mining pool.

Elective AML-KYC

The AML-KYC data are not visible on the blockchain because they are encrypted and stored off-chain. This feature reduces the potential network drag caused by each address carrying increasing amounts of otherwise irrelevant AML-KYC data.

Regulatory Compliance At Will

Information Security

Peace of Mind

A hash of the AML-KYC data is stored on the blockchain but only the recipient address owner can attach the hash to the off-chain data and decrypt its content. This reduces the risk of exposing sensitive information to unrelated parties. For completeness, a sender may abandon its wallet address and create a new one after the transaction.

What is Cryptocurrency?
Cryptocurrency is nothing but a form of digital currency, which is designed to be anonymous and secure. It is associated with the internet, which uses cryptography - the process in which one converts data into a code undecipherable to all but the holder of an alphanumeric key.
